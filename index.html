Speed of entry

A passage in seconds, not minutes.

Tap the meter keyboard. Each tap adds a bar, 5/8, 7/16, 4/4, 3/8, in any order, landing with musically correct accents already in place. For the bars that need more, every subdivision is one tap away.

Ableton Link

Start, bend, and stop the tempo together.

It's not just DAWs. Every player running Tactus starts, stops, and rides tempo from one shared grid, each following their own accents and subdivisions. And because Tactus speaks Ableton Link, that same grid extends to Logic Pro, Ableton Live, and anything else that speaks it.

Starting together is automatic. Staying together through a rit or accel means everyone needs the same tempo map, so Tactus lets you share a passage and import the whole thing, or just its tempo map. At a steady tempo, matching BPM is all it takes.

Frequently asked

Questions, in plain language.

Is this a metronome?
Not really. A metronome repeats one click at a fixed tempo. Tactus maps the rhythmic shape of a specific passage, changing meters, subdivisions, accents, and plays that back. A rehearsal-prep tool, not a drill tool.
Does it work with a DAW?
Yes, via Ableton Link. Join the same WiFi network and tempos sync bidirectionally with zero setup. Optional Start/Stop Sync controls every connected app at once.
Can the tempo change within a passage?
Yes, two ways: per-bar BPM overrides, or the Tempo Shape editor for a gradual ramp across a range of bars.
Does it sync between my iPad and iPhone?
Yes, passages sync via iCloud and appear on all your devices automatically. Nothing beyond your Apple ID required.
Is there a subscription?
No. One-time purchase, everything included, free updates for the lifetime of the major version.
Can I share passages with collaborators?
Yes, export a .rhythmmap file and send it any way you like. They open it directly in their copy of Tactus.
